## 6 FACILITATION TECHNIQUES IMPLEMENTED

### 1. TRAINER GUIDED (Traditional I Do, We Do, You Do)
**Philosophy**: Gradual release of responsibility from trainer to learner

**Structure**:
- **Step 1**: Basic presentation of contents
  - Trainer explains, demonstrates, presents examples
  - Learners observe, take notes, ask questions
  
- **Step 2**: Involving learners to master contents
  - Trainer gives working examples, provides feedback
  - Learners practice with guidance, compare work

**Duration**: 50 minutes
**Resources**: Textbooks, Computers, Projector, Pens, Chalks
**Best For**: Technical skills, procedures, step-by-step processes

---

### 2. BRAINSTORMING (Creative Thinking)
**Philosophy**: Generate maximum ideas without judgment, then organize

**Structure**:
- **Step 1**: Idea Generation Phase
  - Trainer asks open-ended questions, records all ideas
  - Learners generate ideas freely, build on contributions
  
- **Step 2**: Idea Organization and Clustering
  - Trainer guides grouping, identifies patterns
  - Learners categorize ideas, discuss connections
  
- **Step 3**: Application and Implementation
  - Trainer provides practical scenarios
  - Learners apply organized ideas to solve problems

**Duration**: 60 minutes
**Resources**: Whiteboard, Markers, Sticky notes, Flip charts
**Best For**: Problem-solving, creative solutions, innovation

---

### 3. GROUP DISCUSSION (Collaborative Learning)
**Philosophy**: Learning through dialogue and peer interaction

**Structure**:
- **Step 1**: Topic Introduction and Group Formation
  - Trainer presents question, divides into groups (4-6)
  - Learners form groups, prepare materials
  
- **Step 2**: Small Group Discussion
  - Trainer circulates, facilitates without dominating
  - Learners discuss actively, share perspectives, record points
  
- **Step 3**: Whole Class Sharing and Synthesis
  - Trainer invites presentations, summarizes learning
  - Learners present findings, listen, participate

**Duration**: 55 minutes
**Resources**: Discussion guides, Flip charts, Markers, Notebooks
**Best For**: Critical thinking, communication, diverse perspectives

---

### 4. SIMULATION (Realistic Practice)
**Philosophy**: Learn by doing in safe, controlled environment

**Structure**:
- **Step 1**: Simulation Preparation and Setup
  - Trainer explains scenario, assigns roles, provides materials
  - Learners understand context, accept roles, prepare
  
- **Step 2**: Simulation Execution
  - Trainer monitors, observes performance
  - Learners perform roles, apply concepts, interact
  
- **Step 3**: Debriefing and Reflection
  - Trainer leads reflection, connects to theory
  - Learners share insights, relate to real-world

**Duration**: 65 minutes
**Resources**: Simulation materials, Role cards, Scenario sheets, Props
**Best For**: Workplace scenarios, decision-making, soft skills

---

### 5. EXPERIENTIAL LEARNING (Kolb's Cycle)
**Philosophy**: Learning through experience, reflection, and application

**Structure**:
- **Step 1**: Concrete Experience (Doing)
  - Trainer sets up hands-on activity, provides materials
  - Learners engage directly, observe, experiment
  
- **Step 2**: Reflective Observation (Reflecting)
  - Trainer guides reflection, asks probing questions
  - Learners reflect, discuss observations, identify patterns
  
- **Step 3**: Abstract Conceptualization and Active Experimentation
  - Trainer connects theory to practice
  - Learners apply concepts to new situations, practice

**Duration**: 70 minutes
**Resources**: Hands-on materials, Tools, Equipment, Safety gear, Worksheets
**Best For**: Practical skills, laboratory work, hands-on trades

---

### 6. JIGSAW (Cooperative Learning)
**Philosophy**: Each learner becomes expert, teaches others

**Structure**:
- **Step 1**: Expert Group Formation
  - Trainer divides topic into subtopics, assigns groups
  - Learners join expert groups, receive materials
  
- **Step 2**: Expert Group Study and Mastery
  - Trainer supports groups, ensures understanding
  - Learners master subtopic, prepare to teach
  
- **Step 3**: Jigsaw Groups and Peer Teaching
  - Trainer forms mixed groups (one expert each)
  - Learners teach their subtopic, learn from others

**Duration**: 75 minutes
**Resources**: Subtopic materials, Textbooks, Handouts, Notebooks, Markers
**Best For**: Complex topics, peer teaching, comprehensive coverage

## RTB SESSION PLAN STRUCTURE

### Complete Session Flow:
1. **Introduction** (10 min)
   - Greeting, attendance, review previous topic, announce new topic
   
2. **Development/Body** (50-75 min depending on technique)
   - Technique-specific activities with Trainer's and Learner's activities
   
3. **Conclusion** (10 min)
   - Summary, review, connect to objectives
   
4. **Assessment** (3 parts)
   - Summary: Check objectives achieved
   - Assessment/Assignment: Home-based work
   - Evaluation: What worked, what to improve, link to next session

## KEY FEATURES OF IMPLEMENTATION

### 1. Trainer's Activity vs Learner's Activity
Every step clearly separates:
- What the TRAINER does
- What the LEARNER does

### 2. Topic Integration
The chosen topic is woven throughout all activities naturally

### 3. Progressive Complexity
Each technique has 2-3 steps that build on each other

### 4. Appropriate Resources
Resources match the technique:
- Trainer Guided: Traditional classroom tools
- Brainstorming: Creative tools (sticky notes, flip charts)
- Simulation: Role cards, props
- Experiential: Hands-on materials, safety gear
- Jigsaw: Multiple resource sets for subtopics

### 5. Realistic Durations
- Brainstorming: 60 min (needs time for idea generation)
- Trainer Guided: 50 min (efficient direct instruction)
- Group Discussion: 55 min (discussion + sharing)
- Simulation: 65 min (setup + execution + debrief)
- Experiential: 70 min (do + reflect + apply)
- Jigsaw: 75 min (expert groups + jigsaw groups)

## PEDAGOGICAL FOUNDATIONS

### Trainer Guided
- Based on: Gradual Release of Responsibility (Pearson & Gallagher)
- Cognitive Load Theory

### Brainstorming
- Based on: Alex Osborn's Creative Problem Solving
- Divergent/Convergent Thinking

### Group Discussion
- Based on: Social Constructivism (Vygotsky)
- Collaborative Learning Theory

### Simulation
- Based on: Situated Learning Theory
- Authentic Assessment

### Experiential Learning
- Based on: Kolb's Experiential Learning Cycle
- Constructivist Learning Theory

### Jigsaw
- Based on: Aronson's Jigsaw Classroom
- Cooperative Learning (Johnson & Johnson)
